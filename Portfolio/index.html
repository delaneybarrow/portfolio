About Me

Delaney Barrow is a sophomore at University High School in Tucson, Arizona. In addition to her rigorous course load of AP and Honors classes, she plays club and school soccer, is the Vice President of the Unity Club, a group that tutors high school refugees, is Class Officer of Future Business Leaders of America, a participant in Cyber Patriot, a club that holds cyber security competitions, and in her spare time she referees soccer games.
This past summer she attended two computer coding programs “Kode with Klossy”, for which she received a full scholarship, and “Girls Who Code. This further fostered her interest in computer science and love of coding, as well as the cultivation of a new sisterhood. Her biggest technical accomplishment to date was creating an app so people can track their daily water usage in order to encourage conservation. In college, she plans on studying computer science, specifically AI or data science.
Next summer she also plans on joining “Amigos”, an organization that promotes relationships between rural South American communities and North American High School students. She will spend the summer creating and implementing a project in her community in Panama.
