About Bali

Bali is a small island in Indonesia located in the westernmost end of the Lesser Suda Islands. It is part of a province of Indonesia, which includes other small islands around Bali like Nusa Lembonogan and Nusa Penida. Denpasar, is the capitol of Bali, located on its southern end. The major religion of Indonesia is Islam, but on Bali, 83% of its population of 4 million is Hindu.

Recreation

Bali offers some amazing and breathtaking waterfalls around the island. Some are just a short walk away, while others are more of a trek throuhg the jungle. These waterfalls are masterpieces of mother nature. Enjoy the amazing view, or jump in and swim around for an upclose look.

The island has three volcanoes on it, two of which are still active. Mt Agung it the largest volcano, and its peak is the highest point on the island. It has not erupted for 50 years, but currently there are evacuation warnings because there could be another one soon. Mt. Batur on the other hand, is Bali's most popular and more active volcano. Many tourist come to make the early morning trek up the mountain to watch the sunrise from the top. Lastly, Mt Bratan is a large volcano to the east of Bali surrounded by three lakes. It is a densly vegetated area that offers scenic views and hot springs.

Culture

Bali is 83.5% Hindu, but only 1.8% of Indoonesia is Hindu. Because of this the customs of Bali represent a sharp contrast to those of the surrounding islands with a Muslim majority. Bali is said to be primarily Hindu due to the influence of traveling Indian priest from centuries ago. Due to this, people's names on Bali refelct the caste of which they are in. Bali is also known as "the island of 20,000 temples" because it really has more than 20,000 temples on the island. When you travel to Bali, visiting one of these amazing pieces of architecture is a must. But, when visiting a temple it is required that you wear a sarong to cover your legs. Each city on the isalnd also offers a different scraft. One city may be known for it's wood carving, and another their paintings. Hoping around from town to town is a great way to experience Balinese culture first hand. Bali is also know for growning rice. In most towns you can look out and see rice terraces in all directions. Locals still grow their rice traditionally using ox drawn plows and picking the stalks by hand. Bali's dense vegetation is also home to many animals. The Ubud Monkey Forest is a popular tourist attraction. You can buy bananas and feed them to the monkeys in the forset. But, be carefully because they are very competetive and will jump on you. Bali is also known for its Kopi Luwak coffee, the most expensive coffee in the world. Many plantations have Asian palm Civets, which are small nocturnal animals. They feed these animals coffee beans and then coffee is made from the digested cofee beans. This is supposed to create a smooth, rich flavor of coffee. Bali offers a great way to experience a different culture, while still having a fun and relaxing vacation.
